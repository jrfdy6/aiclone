# AGC Operating Model

## Definition

AGC is a government-contracting-first opportunity and capture system for AI consulting.
It does not treat broad consulting sprawl as a substitute for credible market movement.

## Primary Audiences

- Government buyers
- Prime contractors
- Adjacent buyers who can become credible opportunity paths

## Core Loop

1. Signal
   Capture opportunity, market, partner, and buyer signal.
2. Positioning
   Clarify what AGC should stand for in this cycle.
3. Capability
   Turn positioning into a believable capability surface.
4. Surface
   Put the message or material where a real buyer can encounter it.
5. Inbound Email
   Watch for qualified email communication from prospective buyers.
6. Qualified Conversation
   Decide whether the conversation is real enough to advance.
7. Capture
   Record the next move, evidence, and blocker.

## Inputs

- Procurement and buyer signal
- Inbound email communication
- Capability questions
- Partner and market observations
- Website and contact activity
- Opportunity qualification notes

## Outputs

- Positioning briefs
- Capability materials
- Response drafts
- Qualification notes
- Capture-ready next-step summaries

## Machine Cadence

- `5-minute` execution polling
- `30-minute` internal sync and standup-prep loop
- Daily rollups when the sync loop surfaces meaningful change

## Operating Priorities

- Use qualified inbound email communication as the first traction signal
- Keep the business government-contracting first
- Lead with AI consulting
- Keep claims smaller than proof
- Keep the workspace isolated from other workspace memory and execution

## Non-Goals

- Fake certifications, contracts, or past performance
- Broad consulting sprawl before signal justifies it
- Social publishing quotas before the rhythm is stable
- Cross-workspace orchestration
