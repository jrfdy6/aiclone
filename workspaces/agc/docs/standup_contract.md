# AGC Standup Contract

## Purpose

AGC standups exist to review the health of the opportunity and capture system.
They do not exist to center runner churn or automation status unless those issues block execution or visibility.

## Required Standup Structure

Every AGC standup must use these five sections in this order:

1. Signal
2. Work Produced
3. Traction
4. Opportunities
5. Next Focus

## Section Definitions

### 1. Signal

Report:

- the most important buyer, partner, or market signal captured this period
- where it came from
- and what it suggests about AGC positioning

### 2. Work Produced

Report:

- what capability, positioning, response, or qualification artifact changed
- what opportunity it supported
- and whether the artifact is ready, sent, or still blocked

### 3. Traction

Report:

- what qualified `email communication from prospective buyers` occurred
- what type of buyer it came from
- and whether the signal is real enough to move into capture work

Do not confuse light engagement with qualified traction.

### 4. Opportunities

Report:

- what agency, prime, partner, or adjacent opportunity opening became visible
- why it matters
- and what should happen next

### 5. Next Focus

Report:

- the next positioning or capability move
- the next conversation to qualify
- the blocker that most threatens credible progress

## Standup Rules

- Keep the business loop visible: Signal -> Positioning -> Capability -> Surface -> Inbound Email -> Qualified Conversation -> Capture.
- Keep AGC government-contracting first.
- Never invent unsupported capability, compliance status, or past performance.
- Mention automation only when it blocks output, tracking, or decision quality.
- Keep the lane isolated to `agc`.

## Good Standup Outcome

A good AGC standup makes it obvious:

- what the market is saying
- what AGC produced in response
- whether engagement is turning into inbound email
- what opportunity is now worth pursuing
- and what the next capture move should be
