# AGC Agency and Prime Watchlist

This watchlist is the first intentional target map for `agc`.

It is not a claim that AGC already has access, past performance, or live demand from these targets.
It is a prioritization layer built from official public sources and inference about where AGC's government-contracting-first AI consulting posture is most plausible.

## Selection Rule

Targets make this list when all of the following are true:

- the mission or market visibly involves AI, data, workflow modernization, or service operations
- the target is large enough to justify a partner or subcontracting path
- AGC can speak credibly about workflow, knowledge, automation, or trustworthy AI without inventing credentials

## Agency Watchlist

### 1. GSA

Why this is on the list:
- GSA is the gateway for many federal IT purchasing motions and publishes current IT contract vehicles and purchasing programs.

AGC fit hypothesis:
- indirect route first
- useful as an ecosystem and contracting-vehicle anchor more than a pure direct-buyer lane

Best first angle:
- AI consulting framed around workflow modernization, knowledge operations, internal service experience, and low-risk pilot definition

Source:
- GSA IT contract vehicles and purchasing programs: https://www.gsa.gov/technology/it-contract-vehicles-and-purchasing-programs

### 2. HHS

Why this is on the list:
- HHS publicly states that it is investing in and implementing AI across the department and maintains strategy and implementation materials.

AGC fit hypothesis:
- good fit for operational AI, workforce workflow support, knowledge systems, documentation acceleration, and service delivery process work

Best first angle:
- trustworthy AI adoption support around administrative and service workflows

Source:
- Artificial Intelligence at HHS: https://www.hhs.gov/about/agencies/asa/ocio/ai/index.html
- HHS AI Strategy and Implementation: https://www.hhs.gov/programs/topic-sites/ai/strategy-implementation/index.html

### 3. DHS

Why this is on the list:
- DHS has a visible department-level AI posture with concrete workforce and mission use cases.

AGC fit hypothesis:
- strong fit for trustworthy AI implementation, document-heavy workflow improvement, and operational support use cases where governance matters

Best first angle:
- responsible AI workflow support for internal operations or mission-adjacent knowledge work

Source:
- Artificial Intelligence at DHS: https://www.dhs.gov/ai
- DHS S&T AI and Autonomous Systems: https://www.dhs.gov/science-and-technology/artificial-intelligence

### 4. VA

Why this is on the list:
- VA has a Chief AI Officer team, a National AI Institute, and ongoing modernization programs.

AGC fit hypothesis:
- good fit where AI helps reduce administrative burden, improve documentation flow, and support service operations without making clinical claims

Best first angle:
- workflow and knowledge support around Veteran service delivery and administrative efficiency

Source:
- VA Chief AI Officer: https://department.va.gov/ai/chief-ai-officer/
- VA National AI Institute: https://department.va.gov/ai/about/

### 5. Treasury

Why this is on the list:
- Treasury publishes AI strategy resources and has publicly described AI use in fraud detection, risk management, and operationalization.

AGC fit hypothesis:
- strong fit for payment integrity, fraud/risk workflows, knowledge ops, and operational AI consulting

Best first angle:
- AI consulting tied to fraud, risk, and operational process improvement

Source:
- Treasury and Artificial Intelligence: https://home.treasury.gov/policy-issues/financial-markets-financial-institutions-and-fiscal-service/treasury-and-artificial-intelligence
- Treasury enhanced fraud detection with AI: https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/jy2650

## Prime Watchlist

The agency list tells AGC where demand is visible.
The prime list tells AGC where a realistic first partner path may exist.

### 1. Booz Allen

Why this is on the list:
- Booz Allen publicly positions itself as a leading provider of AI services to the U.S. federal government.

Partner angle:
- specialist support on workflow mapping, knowledge-system design, prompt and agent operations, or lightweight AI implementation support under a larger prime-led engagement

Source:
- Booz Allen AI capabilities: https://www.boozallen.com/expertise/artificial-intelligence/ai-solutions.html
- Booz Allen AI services ranking note: https://www.boozallen.com/insights/ai-research/booz-allen-ranks-first-in-artificial-intelligence-services.html

### 2. SAIC

Why this is on the list:
- SAIC publicly emphasizes AI services for national civilian government plus broad federal civilian coverage.

Partner angle:
- targeted subcontracting support where a prime needs narrow AI workflow, documentation, or pilot support instead of another large generalist team

Source:
- SAIC homepage: https://www.saic.com/
- SAIC federal civilian agencies: https://www.saic.com/who-we-serve/federal-civilian-agencies

### 3. Leidos

Why this is on the list:
- Leidos publicly highlights government, health, homeland, intelligence, and science markets.

Partner angle:
- targeted AI consulting support where federal operations and service modernization intersect with larger systems work

Source:
- Leidos markets: https://www.leidos.com/markets

### 4. Accenture Federal Services

Why this is on the list:
- Accenture Federal Services explicitly publishes federal civilian and data-and-AI capabilities.

Partner angle:
- high-volume operations, service delivery, and transformation programs where AGC can support a tighter workflow or operational AI lane

Source:
- Accenture Federal Civilian: https://www.accenture.com/us-en/services/us-federal-government/federal-civilian
- Accenture federal data and AI: https://www.accenture.com/us-en/services/us-federal-government/applied-intelligence-federal

### 5. GDIT

Why this is on the list:
- GDIT publicly emphasizes AI, smart government, citizen services, fraud/waste use cases, and federal civilian mission support.

Partner angle:
- customer-experience, knowledge-work, agentic support, and operational AI use cases where AGC can contribute focused implementation or design capacity

Source:
- GDIT AI capabilities: https://www.gdit.com/capabilities/technology/technologies/artificial-intelligence/
- GDIT Smart Government: https://www.gdit.com/capabilities/mission-expertise/smart-government/

## First Recommendation

AGC should prefer a prime-partner path before a direct-agency path.

Reason:
- AGC can credibly sell AI consulting now
- AGC cannot yet credibly sell deep federal past performance it does not have
- a partner route is more consistent with the current evidence model
