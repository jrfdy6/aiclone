## First-pass draft

Coffee and Convo started because families didn’t need another admissions funnel talk. They needed someone to sit with the mess: the unpaid bills, the silence from the last school, the kid who shuts down on Tuesdays after OT. So we outlawed slide decks. If you walk into the room, you bring a real question or a family you’re trying to help. That’s it.

A typical session sounds nothing like a pitch. We open with, “What’s the conversation you’re afraid to have this week?” Referral partners, parents, even the skeptical uncle get the mic. We map the schedule conflicts, therapies, and custody quirks on butcher paper and decide what part Fusion can actually own. Sometimes the answer is, “We can’t enroll yet, but here’s the specialist who will get you unstuck.” That honesty is what keeps the room full.

Because we treat it like a working session, not a showcase, the referrals that follow are cleaner. Families walk away with a plan they can defend, not a brochure. Counselors see that we’ll translate their notes into action instead of chasing prestige. When people watch us solve real decisions in the open, trust compounds faster than any outbound sequence we could automate.

If you want results like that, stop staging events that pretend everything is fine. Build the room where people can say the quiet part out loud, and make your team prove they can translate it into the next move.
