## First-pass draft

A mom once sat across from me, hands flat on the table, and stopped taking notes mid-sentence. Another school had just promised her son the world and never followed up. She was waiting for me to start reading from the same script.

So I closed the deck, slid my laptop to the side, and asked her what Tuesday mornings actually looked like in their house. No pitch. Just the truth: he hides in the bathroom until the school bus leaves, she emails the counselor, nobody calls them back. That was the first time she looked up.

From there we built clarity, not hype. We mapped every appointment they already juggle, the therapists the family actually trusts, the hours they have to protect because sensory overload wipes him out. Only after that did we talk about how Fusion would plug in and what it would take from us to make the plan real.

People love to say "families just want to feel heard." Nah. They want someone to sit with the mess long enough to name it, then give them a plan they can defend to skeptical grandparents, case managers, and their own kid. The minute you stop pitching and start translating their reality, the anxiety drops and next steps become possible.
