# AI Swag Store Operating Model

## Definition

AI Swag Store is a differentiated merch demand-discovery and offer-testing system for AI-themed physical goods.
It does not optimize for catalog size or social vanity first.

## Primary Audience

- Consumers of swag and merch

## Core Loop

1. Signal
   Capture visits, store behavior, product interest, and meaningful demand clues.
2. Offer
   Turn signal into a clearer concept, product angle, or landing-page hypothesis.
3. Listing
   Publish or refine the product page, bundle, or landing page.
4. Visit
   Watch whether people actually arrive and what they do next.
5. Learn
   Separate useful demand signal from noise.
6. Iterate
   Tighten the offer, replace the weak concept, or make the next test explicit.

## Inputs

- Website visits
- Product-page behavior
- Store and landing-page observations
- Merch concept notes
- Fulfillment constraints
- Repeatable demand patterns

## Outputs

- Offer tests
- Listing and landing-page updates
- Catalog decisions
- Traffic-learning notes
- Standup-ready summaries

## Machine Cadence

- `5-minute` execution polling
- `30-minute` internal sync and standup-prep loop
- Daily rollups when the sync loop surfaces meaningful change

## Operating Priorities

- Use `website visits` as the first traction signal
- Prefer differentiated offers over generic merch filler
- Keep operations simple enough to repeat
- Keep the workspace isolated from other workspace memory and execution

## Non-Goals

- Social publishing quotas before the rhythm is stable
- Catalog sprawl with no signal
- Cross-workspace orchestration
- Generic print-on-demand churn
