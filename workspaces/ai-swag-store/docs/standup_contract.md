# AI Swag Store Standup Contract

## Purpose

AI Swag Store standups exist to review the health of the merch demand loop.
They do not exist to center runner churn or automation status unless those issues block execution or visibility.

## Required Standup Structure

Every AI Swag Store standup must use these five sections in this order:

1. Signal
2. Work Produced
3. Traction
4. Opportunities
5. Next Focus

## Section Definitions

### 1. Signal

Report:

- the most important store, traffic, or demand signal captured this period
- where it came from
- and what it suggests about the current offer

### 2. Work Produced

Report:

- what listing, page, offer, or operational artifact changed
- what test it supported
- and whether the artifact is ready, live, or still blocked

### 3. Traction

Report:

- what happened with `website visits`
- whether the signal is usable or still too thin
- and what changed in traffic quality or store attention

Do not invent traction that is not visible in the artifacts.

### 4. Opportunities

Report:

- what product, traffic, or audience opening became visible
- why it matters
- and what should happen next

### 5. Next Focus

Report:

- the next offer to test
- the next page or listing change to make
- the blocker that most threatens clean learning

## Standup Rules

- Keep the business loop visible: Signal -> Offer -> Listing -> Visit -> Learn -> Iterate.
- Mention social only when it directly improves store learning or traffic.
- Mention automation only when it blocks output, tracking, or decision quality.
- Keep the lane isolated to `ai-swag-store`.

## Good Standup Outcome

A good AI Swag Store standup makes it obvious:

- what the store is learning
- what changed in the offer or store surface
- whether visits are improving
- what opportunity is now visible
- and what the next test should be
