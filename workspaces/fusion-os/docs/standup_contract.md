# Fusion OS Standup Contract

## Purpose

Fusion OS standups exist to review the health of the signal-to-trust system.
They do not exist to center packet state, runner churn, or automation status unless those issues are blocking output.

## Required Standup Structure

Every Fusion standup must use these five sections in this order:

1. Signals Captured
2. Content Produced
3. Audience Response
4. Opportunities Created
5. Next Focus

## Section Definitions

### 1. Signals Captured

Report:

- the most important signals observed this period,
- where they came from,
- and what pattern they suggest.

Acceptable inputs:

- parent concerns
- partner conversations
- internal observations
- events
- market trends

### 2. Content Produced

Report:

- what was produced,
- which audience it served,
- which pillar it belonged to,
- and whether it was drafted, approved, or published.

Minimum weekly expectation:

- 2 family posts
- 2 partner posts

### 3. Audience Response

Report:

- what families responded to,
- what partners responded to,
- what leadership noticed,
- and what changed in understanding, trust, or credibility.

Do not default to vanity metrics without interpretation.

### 4. Opportunities Created

Report:

- what family, partner, or leadership opportunity emerged,
- why it matters,
- and what should happen next.

Opportunity means trust-created opening, not just engagement.

### 5. Next Focus

Report:

- the next narrative focus,
- the next event to frame,
- the next audience gap to clarify,
- and any blocker that threatens weekly output quality.

## Standup Rules

- Use institutional language and business signal, not personality commentary.
- Keep the loop visible: Signal -> Pattern -> Insight -> Narrative -> Distribution -> Trust -> Opportunity.
- Mention automation only when it blocks output, tracking, or decision quality.
- Do not use standup time to restate packet plumbing unless it materially affects delivery.
- Tie PM cards and follow-up work to narrative output, event work, or opportunity creation.

## Good Standup Outcome

A good Fusion standup makes it obvious:

- what the institution is learning,
- what narrative it is producing,
- how the audience is responding,
- what trust is changing,
- and what the next communication move should be.
