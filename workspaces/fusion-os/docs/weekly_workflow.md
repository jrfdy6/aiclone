# Fusion OS Weekly Workflow

## Weekly Objective

Run one full signal-to-trust cycle every week and ship four institutional posts.

## Weekly Deliverables

- 2 family posts
- 2 partner posts
- 1 to 2 event selections
- Pre-event framing for each selected event
- Post-event insight extraction when applicable
- 1 weekly summary

## Monday - Signal Review

- Review parent concerns, partner conversations, internal observations, events, and market trends.
- Cluster signals into patterns.
- Decide which patterns matter to families, which matter to partners, and which matter to leadership.
- Identify 1 to 2 relevant events for the week.

Output:
- signal list
- pattern list
- event list

## Tuesday - Family Narrative

- Convert the strongest family-facing pattern into two family posts.
- Use Observation -> Clarification -> Guidance.
- Keep tone calm, institutional, and useful.

Output:
- family post 1
- family post 2

## Wednesday - Partner Narrative

- Convert the strongest partner-facing pattern into two partner posts.
- Emphasize credibility, clarity, and referral confidence.
- Draft pre-event framing for any event that needs advance positioning.

Output:
- partner post 1
- partner post 2
- pre-event note(s)

## Thursday - Distribution And Review

- Review all four posts for institutional voice and clarity.
- Publish or finalize the weekly content set.
- Check for early audience response from families, partners, and leadership.

Output:
- published or approved weekly content batch
- initial response notes

## Friday - Insight And Summary

- Extract post-event insight if any selected event produced usable signal.
- Record what audience response mattered.
- Record what opportunity was created.
- Decide next week’s focus.

Output:
- post-event insight note(s)
- weekly summary
- next focus

## Weekly Quality Standard

The week is successful when:

- the signal is real,
- the narrative is clear,
- the voice is institutional,
- the content mix matches audience priorities,
- and the summary shows whether trust or opportunity moved.

## Failure Conditions

The week is off-track when:

- content is produced without a clear signal,
- output becomes generic or personality-led,
- families or partners are not both served,
- event work is skipped,
- or standups discuss automation instead of signal, narrative, response, and opportunity.
