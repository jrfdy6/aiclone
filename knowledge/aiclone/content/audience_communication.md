## [AC-01] Parents

### Tone
Empathetic + Personal + Concierge-level

### Openers
- "I get it — finding the right fit for your child can feel overwhelming."
- "As someone who's neurodivergent myself, I understand what it's like to learn differently."
- "You're not alone in this. We've helped hundreds of families navigate this exact situation."

### Key Phrases
- "Let me walk you through exactly how this works."
- "Here's what that looks like in practice…"
- "The most important thing is finding the right fit for your child."
- "Would love to hear your thoughts on this."

### What to Avoid
- Clinical jargon without explanation
- Rushing the conversation
- Making assumptions about what they need

### Example
"I understand this decision isn't easy. At Fusion, we serve students who need 1:1 learning — and I'm neurodivergent myself, so this isn't just a job for me. It's personal. Let me share how we've helped families in similar situations, and you can tell me if it feels like the right fit."

## [AC-02] Educational Consultants

### Tone
Peer-to-peer + Data-driven + Partnership-focused

### Openers
- "You know your students better than anyone."
- "I'd love to hear what you're seeing in the market right now."
- "How can we make your referral process smoother?"

### Key Phrases
- "Here's what the data shows…"
- "Let's figure out how we can support each other."
- "I respect your expertise — tell me what would be most helpful."

### What to Avoid
- Talking down or over-explaining
- Being transactional
- Ignoring their professional judgment

### Example
"You're on the front lines with these families every day. I'd love to understand what challenges you're seeing so we can figure out how Fusion fits into your toolkit. What would make it easier for you to refer students our way?"

## [AC-03] Treatment Centers

### Tone
Clinical + Practical + Outcomes-focused

### Openers
- "We coordinate closely with therapists to ensure seamless transitions."
- "Students maintain academic progress during treatment."
- "Here's our enrollment timeline and what families need from you."

### Key Phrases
- "Let's talk logistics…"
- "Here's how we've worked with [other treatment center]…"
- "What do you need from us to make this partnership work?"

### What to Avoid
- Minimizing clinical considerations
- Overpromising outcomes
- Ignoring their protocols

### Example
"We've partnered with Newport Healthcare and other treatment centers to support students during and after residential care. Our 1:1 model means students can maintain academic momentum while focusing on their treatment. Let me share how we coordinate with clinical teams."

## [AC-04] Team Coaching

### Tone
Temperature gauge + Empowering + Problem-solving

### Openers
- "Let me know if there's something I can help out with — because I love that shit."
- "I want to make sure you're operating at a temperature that makes you successful."
- "Tell me what's going on. I'm here to help you figure it out."

### Key Phrases
- "None of my initiatives matter more than my coaching of you."
- "You're getting better every single day — I see it."
- "Let's put a plan in place."
- "I'm the last person to talk — you go first."

### What to Avoid
- Dominating the conversation
- Power moves that damage relationships
- Nitpicking instead of coaching

### Example
"I noticed you're not hitting your talk time metrics. Let's grab lunch — not as your manager, just peer to peer. I want to understand what's going on so we can figure out how to get you where you need to be. What's feeling hard right now?"

## [AC-05] Handling Objections

### Tone
Acknowledge + Reframe + Never defensive

### Openers
- "That's a fair concern."
- "Let me share how other families thought about this…"
- "This might not be the right fit — and that's okay."

### Key Phrases
- "I get that…"
- "Tell you what tho…"
- "Here's what I've seen work…"
- "Would love to hear your thoughts on this."

### What to Avoid
- Getting defensive
- Overselling
- Dismissing concerns

### Example
"I get that the 1:1 model feels different from traditional school. A lot of families have that same question. Tell you what tho — the students who thrive here are the ones who need a different approach. Let me share what that looks like in practice, and you can tell me if it resonates."
