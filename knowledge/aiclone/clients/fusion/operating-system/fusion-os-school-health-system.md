# Fusion OS School Health System

## Purpose

Fusion OS should not imitate a mature ed-tech enrollment funnel.

It should be an operating system for understanding whether a Fusion campus is healthy, whether future demand is being created, whether package and session mix are strong enough to staff the campus, and what intervention should happen next.

The mature ed-tech archive was still useful because it exposed a repeatable management discipline:

1. measure the system
2. translate numbers into operating reality
3. identify opportunities and risks
4. assign owners
5. escalate only what cannot be solved in-line

Fusion should keep that discipline, but anchor it to its own business model.

## Fusion Operating Truths

- The real unit of business is `scheduled sessions`, not raw student headcount.
- `Full-time` mix is the healthiest core state; registered student count can hide weak package mix.
- `Part-time` packages matter differently in summer, when lower-volume packages are more acceptable.
- Demand is not just generated by marketing. It is actively created through a `pro portfolio` owned by Directors of Admissions.
- A healthy pro relationship produces behavior Fusion actually wants: responses, meetings, event partnership, and referrals.
- A `power-referring` pro is one who refers multiple students in a quarter.
- The real family journey is `inquiry -> intake -> tour -> package -> payment -> scheduling -> attendance`.
- Staffing is driven by `sessions scheduled`, not vanity enrollment counts.
- After a strong tour, the biggest purchase friction is often `cost` and `campus social confidence`.
- A drop in `pro referrals` is an early whisper that a future term may be weak.
- Growth in `full-time student sessions registered` is an early signal that a campus is getting healthier.

## Research-Backed Working Assumptions

These should be treated as current working assumptions until campus-level data replaces them.

### Parent Motivations

- 1:1 personalization and mastery-based pacing
- emotional safety and relief from pressure-cooker school environments
- schedule flexibility with integrated tutoring, mentoring, and support

### Parent Frictions

- cost
- concern that a campus may feel too small socially
- uncertainty about whether the student will feel socially comfortable enough to stay

## Fusion OS Core Domains

### 1. Pro Demand Health

Question: Is future demand being created through a healthy mix of direct inquiry flow and pro-owned relationships?

Signals:

- total owned pros
- unmet pros vs contacted pros
- pro meetings completed
- pro follow-up volume
- referral inquiries
- referred-family tours
- power-referring pros
- concentration risk by top referring segment
- segment mix across private school administrators, consultants, therapists, and treatment centers

### 2. Family Journey And Package Health

Question: Are families moving from inquiry to paid package, and is the package mix aligned to strong-fit outcomes?

Signals:

- inquiry to intake progression
- intake to tour progression
- tours completed
- packages presented
- packages paid
- full-time vs part-time vs tutoring vs mentoring mix
- cost objections
- social-fit objections
- paid-to-scheduled lag

### 3. Session And Staffing Health

Question: Are enough sessions being scheduled in the right mix to support staffing and campus viability?

Signals:

- total sessions scheduled
- full-time sessions registered
- academic vs tutoring vs mentoring session mix
- new vs returning session mix
- time-of-day demand
- subject mix pressure
- teacher utilization
- under-staffing or over-staffing risk

### 4. Campus Social Confidence Health

Question: Is the campus socially credible enough for families to buy and for students to feel this will work?

Signals:

- visible student energy on campus
- same-age peer density
- Homework Cafe participation
- clubs and event participation
- parent confidence in social life
- tour feedback about size or culture
- lost deals due to "too small" concern

### 5. Student Fit, Start, And Retention Health

Question: Are the right students entering, stabilizing, and persisting?

Signals:

- fit-screen quality
- culture-disruption risk
- first-week attendance
- schedule stability after start
- leave or withdrawal patterns
- returner or reactivation behavior
- movement from small packages to broader academic engagement

### 6. Team Execution Health

Question: Can campus and admissions teams run the system consistently without heroics?

Signals:

- Director of Admissions ownership clarity by pro portfolio
- pro follow-up discipline
- tour quality
- package build quality
- scheduling handoff quality
- teacher-staffing coordination
- event execution quality

### 7. Systems And Intelligence Health

Question: Can leadership trust the numbers enough to manage the business correctly?

Signals:

- session source-of-truth trust
- full-time vs part-time mix visibility
- pro-stage hygiene
- referral attribution quality
- dashboard adoption
- manual workaround volume
- definition drift across campuses

## Real Journey Definitions

### Inquiry

A family or a pro raises a hand.

### Intake

Fusion captures the student's context, need state, and early fit signals.

### Tour

The family sees the campus and begins evaluating confidence, cost, and social fit.

### Package

Fusion recommends the academic, tutoring, mentoring, or hybrid package.

### Paid

The family commits financially to the package.

### Scheduled

Sessions are placed on the calendar.

### Stable Start

Working definition for Fusion OS: the student is paid, scheduled, and attending sessions.

## Source-System Crosswalk

Do not carry over old terminology literally. Keep the management discipline and translate it into Fusion's operating reality.

| Mature ed-tech term | Fusion OS translation |
| --- | --- |
| starts | direct or referred inquiries entering the period |
| appointments | tours, family meetings, or pro meetings depending on context |
| submits | completed intake and tour progression |
| completes | package-ready family with fit and pricing clarity |
| admits | fit-confirmed family with a proposed package |
| deposits / enrolls | package paid |
| registers | scheduled sessions, especially full-time sessions registered |
| student success | fit, start stability, and retention |
| LOA / return | leave, pause, returner, or reactivation behavior |
| opportunities | package-mix, pro-portfolio, or session-growth plays |
| risks / escalations | staffing, conversion, culture, or data issues blocking health |

## Core Objects In Fusion OS

### Campus

The operating unit being assessed.

### Scorecard

A normalized seven-domain health snapshot.

### Pro Portfolio

The Director of Admissions-owned book of professionals, segmented by stage and referral strength.

### Family Journey Snapshot

A point-in-time map from inquiry through paid package and scheduled attendance.

### Session Ledger

The current view of scheduled sessions and the mix that drives staffing.

### Package Mix

The distribution of full-time, part-time, tutoring, mentoring, and hybrid packages.

### Risk Flag

A named issue with severity, owner, due date, and unblock condition.

### Intervention

A deliberate action taken to improve a weak domain, stage, or package mix problem.

### Evidence Note

A short factual observation tied to source evidence, not opinion.

## Weekly Operating Rhythm

### Daily

- log pro touches and follow-ups
- update new inquiries, tours, and package decisions
- flag staffing gaps, culture concerns, and schedule issues

### Weekly

- review pro portfolio coverage and meeting activity
- review referral inquiries and referred-family tours
- review package mix and full-time share
- review sessions scheduled and staffing implications
- confirm top three campus risks and intervention owners

### Monthly

- compare trend direction for pro health, package mix, and session health
- review cost and social-fit objection patterns
- review intervention effectiveness
- refresh campus-specific assumptions

### Quarterly

- audit pro concentration risk
- retire weak fields and vanity metrics
- tighten leading indicators
- update campus playbooks and thresholds

## Success Condition

Fusion OS is working when a campus operator can answer these questions quickly:

1. Are we creating enough future demand through pros and direct inquiries?
2. Are families converting into the right package mix?
3. Are we scheduling enough full-time sessions to support staffing?
4. Are cost or social-fit concerns killing good-fit families?
5. What intervention should happen now, and who owns it?
