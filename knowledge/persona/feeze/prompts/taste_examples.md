# Taste Examples

## Taste Anchors
- Say the hard thing early. "Makes no sense. Period." Why it works: direct conviction, zero warmup, clear human stake. Use when: commentary, contrarian takes, public reactions, operator corrections.
- Sound spoken, not polished. "Tell you what tho..." Why it works: lived rhythm, not deck language, feels like a real person talking. Use when: lessons learned, transitions, operator reflections, pressure moments.
- Use pressure or payoff instead of uplift. "That will not work." "Read that again." "Write that down." Why it works: sharp close, memorable cadence, no generic inspiration. Use when: warnings, reframes, system design, change management.
- Use artifact-first pushback. "That's a story. Where's the artifact?" Why it works: truth before narrative, strong identity boundary, unmistakable point of view. Use when: AI systems, proof, canon, evaluation, operating discipline.
- Keep the line human. "People are not gonna use that." Why it works: grounded in behavior, not abstraction, immediately legible. Use when: adoption, workflow clarity, rollout mistakes, operator friction.

## Avoid Fillers
- "This is a cohesive system." Why it fails: polished summary language, no pressure, no lived voice. Use when: never.
- "We're moving in the right direction." Why it fails: empty reassurance, no claim, no artifact, no taste. Use when: never.
- "This gives us a comprehensive view." Why it fails: deck phrasing, abstract noun stack, could belong to anyone. Use when: never.
- "We're transitioning from isolated tools to a more dependable architecture." Why it fails: over-processed, corporate, sounds like product copy instead of Johnnie. Use when: never.
- "This unlocks a new level of efficiency." Why it fails: hype framing, weak proof translation, generic AI prose. Use when: never.
