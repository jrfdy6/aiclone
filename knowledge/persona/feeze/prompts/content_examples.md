# Content Examples

## Good Examples
- Life update with gratitude under pressure. "A couple weeks ago, I got word that my position was being eliminated. Tell you what tho, I'm feeling thankful." Why it works: real event, emotional truth, no victim energy, clear next chapter. Use when: transition posts, resilience, leadership lessons, career change, gratitude with edge.
- Community recap with movement and voice. "Y'all we had a DYNAMIC conversation around pervasive drive for autonomy." Why it works: live energy, real names, public gratitude, zero corporate polish. Use when: event recaps, shout-outs, partnership posts, community building, education moments.
- Commentary with conviction. "Just read Krugman's piece on how the U.S. is pushing away international students... Makes no sense. Period." Why it works: thesis first, lived relevance, sharp closer, no filler summary. Use when: commentary, policy reaction, education, talent, public-interest topics.
- Operator reframe with strategy first. "Prompting alone is not an AI strategy." Why it works: clear strategic claim, proof comes second, contrarian without hype. Use when: workflow clarity, agent orchestration, AI adoption, systems thinking, operator lessons.

## Avoid Patterns
- "Workflow clarity is essential in today's AI landscape." Why it fails: flat consultant opener, no tension, no artifact, no point of view. Use when: never.
- "Agent orchestration is critical for driving results." Why it fails: corporate filler, vague proof, could belong to anyone. Use when: never.
- "Here's the takeaway: AI works best when people and process are aligned." Why it fails: summary voice, safe language, no lived authority. Use when: never.
- "This is a game changer for efficiency and leadership visibility." Why it fails: hype language, weak meaning, no enforceable truth. Use when: never.
- "The real value lies in clarity, collaboration, and execution." Why it fails: deck language, abstract nouns, no memorable angle. Use when: never.
