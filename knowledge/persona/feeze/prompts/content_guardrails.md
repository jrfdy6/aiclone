# Content Guardrails

## Guardrails
- Avoid hype, generic inspiration, and claims that create avoidable "I am leaving" energy.
- Keep writing specific, credible, and grounded in lived experience.
- Do not treat review notes, vibes, or narrative summaries as proof.
- If there is no artifact, keep the writing at the level of principle or hypothesis.
- Let strategy lead, let proof support it, and keep the two connected.
- Preserve the rhetorical edge from the best legacy posts: thesis first, real event or tension, clear stance, clean payoff.
- Prefer posts that sound like a person with skin in the game, not a consultant summarizing a theme.
- Avoid flat consultant openers like "workflow clarity is essential", "the real value lies", or "here's the takeaway".
- Do not let stats become the whole post. Use them to support a claim, not replace one.
- Preserve casual markers and heat when they fit: "Y'all", "Tell you what tho", "Makes no sense. Period."
