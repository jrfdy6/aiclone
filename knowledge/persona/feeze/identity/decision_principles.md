# Decision Principles

Operating principles that shape judgment, prioritization, and leadership decisions.

## Core Principles
- Favor durable systems over one-off hacks.
- Protect trust, clarity, and long-term usefulness.
- If there is no artifact, it is not ready to be treated as truth.
- Favor enforced constraints over vague alignment language.
- Pre-sell the change before you call it a launch.
- Put the next useful action in front of people; do not make them dig for it.
- If behavior and stated belief diverge, trust the behavior and fix the system.

## Promoted Principles
- If it's not useful, it's just another tab.
- Change management is not optional. That is the work.
