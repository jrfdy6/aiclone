## FEEZIE OS

### What It Is For

`FEEZIE OS` is the public-facing operating system for Feeze's visibility, personal brand, and career positioning.

It starts with LinkedIn, but it is not meant to stay trapped as "just a LinkedIn workspace."

Its job is to:
- convert lived work into public signal
- preserve persona truth while building thought leadership
- connect current work, operating lessons, and long-term narrative
- create a recognizable public story around who Feeze is and what Feeze is building

### What Outcomes Matter Most

- public signal stays grounded in real work, not generic posting
- the system tells Feeze's story better over time
- source signals and lived reactions become reusable assets
- visibility supports business credibility, career leverage, and future opportunities
- the lane becomes an honest public operating system rather than a content treadmill

### What It Should Never Become

- AI slop
- vanity posting
- fake founder performance
- empty engagement bait
- generic "build in public" cosplay detached from real life and real work
- a flattened identity that ignores the range of lanes Feeze actually lives in

### Success Markers

#### 30 Days

- FEEZIE OS is embedded into the normal workflow.
- Posts and reactions come from real signal instead of blank-page ideation.
- The voice starts to feel clearly human, grounded, and recognizable.

#### 90 Days

- The lane reliably turns real work into public assets.
- The content system is supporting career narrative and business credibility.
- The PM board, standups, and persona canon are all reinforcing the lane instead of fragmenting it.

#### 180 Days

- FEEZIE OS is broader than LinkedIn while still coherent.
- The system can represent Feeze across leadership, operations, education, AI, therapy/referral, entrepreneurship, and style without collapsing everything into one brand cliche.
- The public story is durable, compounding, and clearly tied to lived proof.

## Workspace Priorities

### Fusion OS

- Primary goal: build an operating system for admissions, enrollment, referral systems, school operations, and leadership execution that protects trust and makes execution clearer.
- Current stage: planned, with direction clearer than execution detail.
- Top priorities:
  - define the local operating model for admissions, enrollment, and referral work
  - capture frontline friction and use it to improve process
  - create SOP-quality clarity before trying to scale anything
- Biggest blocker: local goals, source artifacts, and day-to-day operating truth are not yet rich enough in the system.
- Optimize for: trust, clarity, and repeatability.

### EasyOutfitApp

- Primary goal: improve recommendation quality through stronger metadata, context-aware outfit logic, and product feedback loops.
- Current stage: planned product lane with a clear quality philosophy.
- Top priorities:
  - strengthen metadata quality
  - enforce context-aware outfit validation
  - turn user feedback into concrete product improvement
- Biggest blocker: the lane still needs more real product signal and operating truth than broad product intent.
- Optimize for: recommendation quality that is legible, explainable, and measurably better.

### AI Swag Store

- Primary goal: test demand for AI-branded physical goods without creating generic merch clutter.
- Current stage: planned commerce lane.
- Top priorities:
  - validate demand before expanding catalog
  - define a repeatable drop / merch-test workflow
  - keep fulfillment and operations simple enough to repeat
- Biggest blocker: not enough real demand signal yet.
- Optimize for: low-risk demand validation, signal-backed creative, and operational simplicity.

### AGC

- Primary goal: hold a distinct operating slot for AGC so the mission stays separate from surrounding noise.
- Current stage: planned lane with structural intent but limited concrete operating detail.
- Top priorities:
  - clarify the initiatives that actually belong in AGC
  - give each initiative its own traceable operating model
  - keep AGC from being treated like a generic misc bucket
- Biggest blocker: the mission is still underdefined compared with the stronger lanes.
- Optimize for: separation, traceability, and mission clarity.

## Brain Routing Defaults

### Default Pattern For A Strong Signal

Default route:
- `canonical memory + standup`

Why:
- strong signals should usually be remembered first
- then interpreted in the right meeting before becoming execution

### Route To Persona Canon When

- the signal changes identity, voice, worldview, story, principles, or content posture
- the signal is durable enough to affect how the system should represent Feeze

### Route To Canonical Memory When

- the signal changes what is currently true
- the signal should affect crons, standups, and future sessions
- the system should remember it even if no task is created immediately

### Route To Standup When

- the meaning is not obvious yet
- multiple workspaces might be affected
- strategic interpretation is needed from `Neo`, `Jean-Claude`, and `Yoda`

### Route To PM When

- the next step is concrete
- ownership is clear
- the work should move now instead of waiting for more reflection

### Common Combinations

- `persona canon + canonical memory`
- `canonical memory + standup`
- `standup + PM`
- sometimes `persona canon + canonical memory + FEEZIE OS standup`

The system should not assume one source equals one destination.

## Executive Interpretation Rule

For source material that affects direction, the core conversation should be:

- `Yoda`: what does this mean for the long-term direction, personal brand, or owner trajectory?
- `Neo`: what does this change across the system or multiple workspaces?
- `Jean-Claude`: what should be operationalized now, and where?

That executive interpretation should happen before the system turns strong source material into broad downstream changes.
