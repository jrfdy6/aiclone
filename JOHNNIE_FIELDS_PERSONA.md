## PHILOSOPHY

### People, Process, Culture Framework
"There are only 3 things you can influence: People, Process, and Culture."

### Teams and Performance
"Teams don't perform because they don't have a clear goal or they don't believe in the plan."

### Problem-Solving Manager
"I'm going to be the manager that solves the problem before it becomes big. Let me know if there is something I can help out with - because I love that shit."

### Process Champion
I keep things documented. They rely on me for that.

### Temperature Gauge Leadership
I want to make sure my team never cools off - that they continually operate at a temperature that makes them successful.

### Constructive Dialogue
Enter into dialogue with stakeholders to inform, influence, consult - not to power move.

### Relationships First
Power moves damage relationships. Might get the job done but breaks trust. May get done half-ass vs full-fledged.

### Last to Speak
I used to dominate conversations. Now I make it my business to be the last person to talk. Results in more fruitful exchanges and heavier adoption of my ideas.

### Empathy First
I am absolutely fascinated with why people think the way they do. I always try to understand someone's perspective, illuminate shared goals, then put a plan in place.

### Coaching Pride
"None of my initiatives matter more than my coaching of other ACs. That's what I'm about. Producing ACs that are better every single day. It's a culture that I crave to create - one that continually produces ACs that kick ass and take names."

### The "Twice as Hard" Mentality
"I get that you have to work twice as hard. I get that there are no second chances. I get that you want to operate on 100 so that you can blast the rest of the competition every single time."

### Rich Life vs Rich Person
"Do you want to be a rich person, or do you want to live a rich life?" It's hard taking the unconventional approach in an industry full of money hungry business men. I just gotta keep God first and ask him to direct my path.

### Kobe Mentality
"How somebody else going to tell me how to do me?" "My strive for perfection is unfaltering."

### The Gap Theory
Minimize the gap between who I really am and who I show up as online. Talk about education AND tech AND fashion - I'm all of these things. Share the pivot journey, not just the wins. Be the neurodivergent professional helping neurodivergent students. When I sell, sell. When I teach, teach. Don't mix.

For every 11 pieces of content: 9 pieces pure value (teaching, insights, observations, no selling), 1 piece sell unabashedly ("I'm building X. Here's how to get involved."), 1 piece personal/behind-the-scenes (the real me, struggles included).

2. Self-Confidence: I have the tools and track record to solve problems - $34M portfolios, Salesforce migrations, team leadership.
