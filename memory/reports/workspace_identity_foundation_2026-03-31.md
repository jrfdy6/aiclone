# Workspace Identity Foundation - 2026-03-31

This is the first executive foundation pass for workspace identity packs.

It is structured as:
- `Neo` project-wide read
- `Jean-Claude` operating standard
- `Yoda` strategic review

The goal is not final polish.
The goal is a usable foundation the system can iterate on.

## Neo Read

### What Neo sees across the project

- The project already has a strong base identity layer at the root.
- The executive hierarchy is now coherent enough to encode directly:
  - `Neo` as all-system operator
  - `Jean-Claude` as workspace president and execution manager
  - `Yoda` as strategic overlay
- The PM board, standups, Chronicle, and delegated workspace lanes are real enough that identity packs can now influence actual behavior.
- The main remaining risk is not missing files. It is stale or inconsistent language leaking back in through runners, PM defaults, and older artifacts.

### Project-wide pattern Neo extracted

The best workspace documents in this project are:
- short
- explicit
- lane-bounded
- grounded in actual behavior
- clear about what the lane is not
- strong on constraints

The weakest workspace documents are:
- placeholder-only
- too generic
- overly defensive
- disconnected from real workflow, PM truth, or standup behavior

### Neo conclusion

Workspace packs should not read like branding exercises.
They should read like compact operating systems.

The right standard is:
- `IDENTITY.md` says who the lane is
- `SOUL.md` says how it thinks
- `USER.md` says what Feeze wants from it
- `CHARTER.md` says what success and constraints look like
- `AGENTS.md` says what to read and how to operate

### Neo handoff to Jean-Claude

Jean-Claude should:
- normalize workspace pack quality
- keep names aligned across registry, PM defaults, UI, and runners
- use the packs as real inputs for SOP generation
- refuse generic placeholder behavior when the owner has already supplied a real brief

## Jean-Claude Standard

### Operating rule

Every workspace pack should be good enough that Jean-Claude can hand a PM card to the lane and trust the lane to:
- read the right files
- stay in scope
- protect the right constraints
- report back clearly

### What Jean-Claude is standardizing

- executive packs for `Neo`, `Jean-Claude`, and `Yoda`
- real workspace packs for:
  - `FEEZIE OS`
  - `Fusion OS`
  - `EasyOutfitApp`
  - `AI Swag Store`
  - `AGC`

### Workspace-by-workspace foundation

#### FEEZIE OS
- direct executive lane
- current public visibility system
- LinkedIn first, broader public narrative second
- should optimize for persona truth, live source signal, and reusable visibility assets

#### Fusion OS
- operator for admissions, enrollment, school operations, referral systems, and leadership execution
- should protect trust with families and partners
- should use frontline signals to improve process
- should make execution clearer before scaling it

#### EasyOutfitApp
- product lane for outfit logic, metadata quality, user feedback, and style-system refinement
- should privilege metadata and context over vague style language
- should make recommendation quality legible to the user

#### AI Swag Store
- commerce lane for merchandising, product drops, and demand testing
- should test demand before expanding the catalog
- should avoid generic AI merch filler
- should keep operations repeatable

#### AGC
- dedicated slot for AGC with its own operating model
- should separate mission from noise
- should keep decisions traceable back to goals
- should avoid becoming a generic overflow workspace

### Jean-Claude conclusion

The packs are no longer just shells.
They now contain enough operating signal to support:
- executive standups
- SOP creation
- workspace delegation
- PM interpretation

The next Jean-Claude job is not more scaffolding.
It is making future standups and handoffs consume these packs consistently.

## Yoda Review

### What Yoda cares about

The packs should not only help agents execute.
They should help the system remember who Feeze is becoming.

That matters most in:
- `FEEZIE OS`
- the relationship between public signal and real work
- the connection between projects and long-term direction

### Yoda's read on the portfolio

- `FEEZIE OS` is the narrative spine.
- The other workspaces should support real capability, credibility, leverage, or experimentation.
- If a workspace pack becomes too generic, it stops being a second-brain lane and becomes a task bucket.
- The documents must preserve:
  - your voice
  - your standards
  - your priorities
  - your long-range direction

### Yoda conclusion

These packs should make the system easier to align, not just easier to automate.

Good identity packs help the agents answer:
- why this workspace matters
- what it should protect
- what it should never fake
- how it contributes to the larger story

## Foundation Decision

The agents can create a first-pass foundation for these files themselves if:
- they read the real project
- they stay grounded in owner-supplied goals
- they avoid pretending undefined strategy is already known

That is now good enough to proceed with iterative refinement.

3. Let Yoda refine the FEEZIE OS lane further as the public system sharpens.
